About Me

I'm Ryan. I make games, apps, websites (even this one), and more!

My story isn't unique by any means, unfortunately. I have been exposed to all the same experiences as any other programmer. Go down the list and you'll see endless stories about motivation and the restless desire to "create something from nothing" and the usual fascination with technology, and well, it's like all software developers think the same way.

That aside, we don't all act the same way. We all want to do big things, and who doesn't? However, keep dreaming, and you'll never do. That's why I've put some of my ideas down into software so far. I don't just talk about what would be cool to make or do- I act. Over in my Projects tab, you'll see a few examples of what I've worked on, and what I plan to work on.

I think together, we could act together and do something great. Get in touch.

My Projects


Hallowed Island (October 2021)

Made as a one-week challenge for Halloween, this 3D first person shooter game features three uniquely scripted bosses, a huge environment, and an hour experience through a dungeon.

Otaku Puzzle (July 2021)

Made as a response to a friend's challenge about "easy to make puzzle games", this spin on classic 2D puzzles games is 3D, top-down, includes full networking support through WebSockets, implements a search engine media scraper, and creates puzzles that support videos, GIFs, and most formats.

Update Launcher (July 2021)

Made in a few hours with Visual Studio after realizing Otaku Puzzle needed a system that fetched the most recent update, and applied it. This was fairly simple, and includes a tutorial on using it for your own apps.

Good Guy Knight (June 2020)

Made in around two weeks during downtime in the summer, this is a 2D side scroller that follows a sarcastic storyline, and should take around twenty minutes to finish.
